01 ABSTRACT

Beyond noun matching.

Text-query universal sound separation allows users to extract a target sound source using natural language. However, most existing methods understand text only through noun matching: entity nouns in a query—such as “cat meow” or “piano”—are aligned with fixed sound categories. This paradigm fails when instructions require reasoning, as in “extract the brightest-sounding source,” and provides no fine-grained control over sound attributes.

We introduce Universal Sound Separation with Implicit Reasoning Text Query, a new task setting in which the target must be inferred from descriptive, comparative, or attribute-based instructions rather than an explicit category name. We further propose Listen, Think and Separate (ThinkSep), a generative framework that performs instruction understanding, target selection, and separation as one unified process.

ThinkSep tokenizes audio with a four-codebook X-Codec tokenizer and uses a Qwen3-0.6B language model to autoregressively generate target audio tokens in a decoder-only manner, conditioned on the mixture and text instruction. A single framework models semantics and the attributes of speech, music, and general sound, showing that semantic and attribute information are complementary. Across objective and subjective evaluations, ThinkSep achieves state-of-the-art performance on most metrics for both category and attribute-constrained queries.
